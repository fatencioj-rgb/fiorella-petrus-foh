Viticulture

The grapes are sourced from 20 to 40-year-old vines planted on sandy alluvial and metamorphic schist soils in the O Rosal subzone, the southernmost area of Rías Baixas where the Miño river meets the Atlantic Ocean. The mild Atlantic climate, free from dramatic temperature swings, provides ideal conditions for slow, even ripening of indigenous white varieties. All fruit is hand-harvested to ensure only healthy, optimally ripe clusters reach the winery.

Winemaking

Upon arrival at the winery, the grapes are destemmed and given a cold soak to extract primary aromatic compounds before fermentation begins. Fermentation takes place in stainless steel tanks at a controlled temperature of approximately 18 °C over roughly 20 days, preserving freshness and varietal expression. The wine then rests on its fine lees to build texture and complexity. No oak is used at any stage, keeping the focus on purity of fruit and mineral character.

Tasting Notes

Pale yellow in colour with greenish reflections. The nose is aromatic and intense, offering layers of citrus (grapefruit and lemon) alongside stone fruit such as white peach and nectarine, lifted by delicate floral hints and a distinctive saline mineral undertone. On the palate the wine is fresh and structured, with vibrant acidity driving flavours of ripe citrus and a subtle mineral salinity. The finish is medium-long with a pleasant, slightly bitter edge that invites another sip.

Vintage 2024

Galicia experienced a wet spring followed by a temperate summer with moderate rainfall, conditions that preserved natural freshness and acidity in Atlantic white varieties. Harvest was slightly delayed in some plots to achieve optimal phenolic ripeness.
