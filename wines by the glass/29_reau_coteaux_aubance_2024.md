## The Producer

Nicolas Réau is a core member of the Loire Valley's natural wine movement, a community of winemakers who reject industrial farming and heavy-handed winemaking in favour of something more honest. His wines are naturally farmed and naturally fermented, with minimal intervention at every stage.

The Coteaux de l'Aubance is a small, relatively obscure appellation that produces exclusively sweet wines from Chenin Blanc, one of the world's most versatile grapes. While Chenin can make everything from bone-dry to sparkling to unctuously sweet, it is in these sweet expressions that it reveals something magical: an ability to balance richness with razor-sharp acidity, sweetness with freshness. This is not a cloying dessert wine. It is sweet wine that makes you want another glass.

## Viticulture

Vineyards on schist and limestone soils in the Aubance valley. Naturally farmed with no herbicides. Grapes are hand-harvested at late-harvest ripeness, when sugars have concentrated naturally on the vine through extended hang time and, when conditions allow, noble rot.

## Winemaking

Direct pressing of the late-harvested grapes. Fermentation with natural yeasts and minimal intervention. Fermentation stops naturally before all sugar is converted, retaining the residual sweetness that defines the appellation. No heavy manipulation; the focus is on purity and terroir expression.

## Tasting Notes

Distinguished by aromas of candied fruit and honey, with a beautiful freshness that prevents any cloying sensation. The palate shows concentrated sweetness balanced by the bright, natural acidity of Chenin Blanc, with nuances of citrus, quince and stone fruit. A wine of elegance rather than weight.
