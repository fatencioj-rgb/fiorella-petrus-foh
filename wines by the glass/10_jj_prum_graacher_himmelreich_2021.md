## The Producer

J.J. Prüm is the quiet conscience of the Mosel. In a wine world obsessed with power, extraction and newness, this estate has spent generations doing the opposite: making wines of extraordinary lightness, precision and seemingly effortless beauty. Only Riesling is grown. Only traditional methods are used. The wines are never loud.

The estate has been in the Prüm family for generations and is currently managed by Dr. Katharina Prüm. They hold 14 hectares in the most revered sites of the Middle Mosel, including Wehlener Sonnenuhr and Graacher Himmelreich. The name "Himmelreich" means "Kingdom of Heaven," and while this is likely a reference to the vineyard's elevated position, it is hard not to hear it as a description of what the wine itself can be in great years.

The Kabinett level is, in many ways, what J.J. Prüm does best. At just 8% alcohol, these are wines that achieve more with less: impossibly delicate, yet structured enough to age for decades.

## Viticulture

The Graacher Himmelreich is a VDP.Grosse Lage (Grand Cru equivalent). The soils are grey Devonian slate (grauer Devonschiefer), which absorbs heat during the day and radiates it back to the vines during cool nights, contributing the hallmark flinty minerality of Mosel Riesling. The steep slopes ensure excellent drainage and optimal sun exposure.

## Winemaking

The estate adheres to traditional Mosel winemaking methods. Fermentation is entirely spontaneous, using indigenous yeasts, and takes place in traditional wooden Fuder barrels (1,000-litre oval casks). The slow, cool fermentation stops naturally, retaining a delicate balance of residual sugar and racy acidity that defines the Kabinett style. No chaptalization or manipulation is employed.

## Tasting Notes

The bouquet is bright and expressive, with a complex array of apple, pear, bee pollen, iris blossoms, elderflower and a beautiful base of slate. There is the classic smoky, fumy Prüm style with a deep citrus core. On the palate the wine is superbly focused and straight, with flavours of white peach, lime, yuzu, anise and minty herbs, juicy and rounded in texture despite its light body. The acidity is electric and caressing, leading to a very focused, long and mouthwatering finish.

## Vintage 2021

2021 was the vintage that Mosel Riesling lovers had been waiting for. After a string of warm years that pushed alcohol levels up and acidity down, 2021 returned to a pattern many thought was gone forever. A cool, wet growing season (with rain and even flooding during summer) meant grapes ripened extremely slowly, finally reaching maturity only in October and November. Farmers had to return to an era where ripeness was sought out rather than slowed down. The payoff: acid levels absolutely unheard of this decade, combined with moderate must weights perfect for the Kabinett style. James Suckling awarded a perfect 100 points to a 2021 Kabinett from the Mosel, calling it "ideal for the category." Vinous titled their report "Tantalizing, Tingling, Thrilling."
