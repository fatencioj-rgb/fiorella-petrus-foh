## The Producer

The Chave family has been making wine in Hermitage for 16 generations, tracing their lineage back to 1481. That is not a misprint. Over 540 years of unbroken family winemaking on the same hill. If any estate on earth can claim to understand its terroir completely, it is this one.

Hermitage is considered the spiritual home of the Syrah grape, and Domaine Jean-Louis Chave is widely regarded as its greatest interpreter. The domaine holds parcels across multiple lieux-dits on the Hermitage hill, including granitic slopes, clay sections, and alluvial flats. Each parcel is fermented and aged separately until the final blending, which is the moment when the domaine's generations of knowledge come together: knowing exactly how much of each site to use, how each will evolve, and how together they create something greater than the sum of their parts.

The wines are never loud. They are built on purity, minerality and a patience that only 16 generations can teach. At nearly 30 years old, this 1997 is now at a magnificent point of maturity, revealing layers that were invisible in its youth.

## Viticulture

Multiple lieux-dits on the Hermitage hill. Steep, south-facing slopes with varied soils of granite, clay, limestone and alluvial deposits. Each plot contributes different facets to the final blend. The diversity of terroirs within a single appellation is what gives Chave Hermitage its legendary complexity.

## Winemaking

Each parcel fermented and aged separately in a mix of concrete, stainless steel and oak vats. Some grapes are destemmed, but a proportion of whole bunches is included for aromatic complexity and structural lift. The finished wine matures for approximately 18 months in oak barrels, with around 10% new oak, a restrained approach that allows terroir and Syrah character to dominate.

## Tasting Notes

At nearly 30 years of age, this wine has evolved into a complex and deeply layered expression of Hermitage Syrah. Concentrated aromas of blackberry, cassis, violet, smoky minerality, black pepper and herbs, alongside tertiary notes of leather, truffle, dried flowers and earth. The palate is powerful yet refined, with resolved tannins and remarkable length.

## Vintage 1997

The 1997 vintage in the northern Rhône was a ripe, generous year that delivered wines of approachable richness. It was not one of the region's monumental vintages (those honours go to 1990, 1998, 1999 and 2003), but it provided conditions that rewarded experienced winemakers. For Chave, whose multi-parcel approach and generational knowledge allow adaptation to any conditions, 1997 produced a wine of real depth and longevity. Now at nearly 30 years of age, it has reached a point of maturity where tertiary complexity (leather, truffle, dried flowers, earth) has fully developed while the fruit core remains intact. This is what Chave means when they say their wines are "built on patience."
