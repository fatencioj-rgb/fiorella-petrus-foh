## The Producer

Justino's is one of Madeira's oldest and largest producers, founded in 1870. In a region where many of the most famous houses are tiny and rarefied, Justino's fills an important role: making serious, well-crafted Madeira at accessible levels. Their 5 Year Reserve is an introduction to the Madeira style, designed to show what makes this island's wines unique without requiring the commitment (or price) of a 20-year-old single variety.

The "Rich" designation indicates a medium-sweet style, the most approachable entry point for those new to Madeira. But even at this level, you can taste the hallmarks of the style: the warmth from the fortification, the caramelised character from the ageing process, and crucially, the acidity. That acidity is what makes Madeira Madeira; it prevents the sweetness from ever feeling heavy and gives even a 5-year-old wine a freshness that is surprising for its style.

## Winemaking & Ageing

After fermentation on the volcanic island, the wine is fortified with grape spirit. Ageing uses the canteiro method (natural warming in heated lofts) or estufagem (heated tanks) for a minimum of 5 years. The heat accelerates the development of the characteristic oxidised, caramelised flavours.

## Tasting Notes

Amber colour. Aromas of dried fruit, caramel, toffee and toasted nuts. Medium-sweet on the palate with warm, rounded flavours of raisin, burnt sugar and subtle spice. Good acidity provides balance and freshness. Clean, warming finish.
