## The Producer

Gimonnet-Gonet is the union of two Champagne dynasties. Anne Gonet and Philippe Gimonnet, each born into families who have made Champagne for generations, released their first bottles together in 1986. Their son Charles joined in 2012 and daughter Anne-Sophie in 2020, continuing what has become a generational pursuit of great Champagne.

What makes this house distinctive is its deep roots in the Côte des Blancs. Nearly all of their 13.5 hectares lie in the Grand Cru villages of Chouilly, Cramant, Oiry, Oger and Le Mesnil-sur-Oger. This is Chardonnay country, and the vast majority of their range reflects that identity: precise, mineral-driven blanc de blancs.

Which is exactly why this wine exists. They call it "L'Étonnant" (the surprising one), and the name is not accidental. In a portfolio built almost entirely on white grapes, this is their sole Blanc de Noirs, a deliberate departure sourced from Pinot Noir and Meunier parcels in the Vallée de la Marne. It is their way of showing that a house defined by Chardonnay finesse can also speak the language of Pinot with equal conviction.

## Viticulture

The fruit comes from carefully selected Pinot Noir and Pinot Meunier parcels in the Vallée de la Marne, where warmer exposures and mixed chalk-clay soils lend both structure and aromatic depth. These are established vineyards with the maturity to produce grapes of concentration and complexity, quite different in character from the family's Côte des Blancs holdings.

## Winemaking

A Blanc de Noirs by definition: a white Champagne made exclusively from black-skinned grapes. The pressing must be gentle and precise to extract clean juice without colour from the skins. The base wine undergoes traditional method secondary fermentation in bottle, followed by extended lees ageing that builds the creamy texture and biscuity depth. The blend includes a high volume of Grand Cru-level fruit, giving this wine a pedigree that outclasses many big-name Champagnes at higher price points.

## Tasting Notes

Of rare elegance, this Blanc de Noirs brings together finesse, structure and depth in a remarkably precise balance. The nose is rich and ripe, with red berry fruit, stone fruit and a subtle breadth that comes from the Pinot Noir dominance. On the palate there is body and generosity, balanced by fresh, zesty acidity and chalky minerality. The mousse is fine and persistent. It is both delicious and surprising: proof that Gimonnet-Gonet's mastery extends well beyond their Chardonnay heartland.
