## The Producer

Few producers anywhere have done more for a single grape variety than Cantina Terlan has done for Pinot Bianco. While the grape is often regarded as a workhorse in Alsace and earns modest respect as Weissburgunder in Germany, Cantina Terlan has gone to extraordinary lengths to explore its full potential, treating it with the seriousness and ambition usually reserved for Chardonnay or Riesling.

Vorberg is the proof. Named after the vineyard on the south-facing slopes of Monzoccolo, it was one of the first single-vineyard Pinot Bianco wines ever produced in Alto Adige and remains a benchmark decades later. The cooperative's 150-plus members farm approximately 190 hectares, but it is the specific volcanic terroir of the Vorberg site, and the patience to age the wine properly before release, that sets this apart from anything else made from this grape variety in the world.

## Viticulture

The Vorberg vineyard sits at altitudes between 450 and 650 metres on steep, south-facing slopes of Monzoccolo. The soils consist of volcanic quartz-porphyry bedrock overlaid with sandy, loamy material rich in quartz and high mineral content. The red porphyric rocks retain the warmth of the day, while the porous soil provides just enough moisture for the roots to extend deep into the mineral-rich subsoil. Warm sun, cool mountain air: the daily tension between the two is what gives Terlan wines their trademark balance of richness and freshness.

## Winemaking

Grapes are manually harvested and carefully selected. Whole clusters undergo gentle pressing, followed by natural sedimentation for must clarification. Fermentation is slow and temperature-controlled in large traditional oak barrels (70 hl and 30 hl). The wine undergoes full malolactic fermentation and is then aged on its lees in traditional wooden barrels for 12 months, building the creamy texture and complexity that define the Vorberg Riserva.

## Tasting Notes

Intense, bright straw yellow with a light golden sheen. The multifaceted nose presents ripe yellow fruit (honeydew melon, Golden Delicious apple, white peach, quince jelly), alongside camomile, pineapple, hints of cold smoke, crushed oystershell and flint. On the palate the wine is creamy and mid-weight, with a sweet fruit texture balanced by mountain freshness. Refined and polished, with flavours of pie crust, roasted almond and citrus zest, leading to a long, mineral-driven finish.

## Vintage 2022

In Alto Adige, 2022 began with a very dry and mild winter. A cool spring slightly delayed vine budding, but a very warm and sunny May restored the normal growth cycle, with rapid flowering. Then came the broader Italian drought: an unprecedented heatwave melted Alpine snowpack by end of May, and rain was absent for over 100 days. A hot spell in August briefly slowed ripening. However, abundant rainfall at the end of August broke the drought, and the weather remained stable and very fine through the fall. The official Alto Adige vintage report cites "good sugar levels, below-average acidity, and very healthy grapes" as keywords for 2022. For Cantina Terlan's high-altitude Vorberg site (450-650m), the mountain breezes and elevation mitigated the heat more effectively than for lower vineyards.
