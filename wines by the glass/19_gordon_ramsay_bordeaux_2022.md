## The Producer

This wine exists because of a specific need: Restaurant Gordon Ramsay, the three-Michelin-star flagship in Chelsea, wanted a house Bordeaux that could sit credibly alongside some of the world's finest wines on their list while remaining accessible and restaurant-ready. It is not a vanity label. Ramsay's name goes on it because the wine is made to his specification, designed to complement fine dining rather than compete with it.

The wine is produced from selected vineyards within the Bordeaux region under the guidance of a winemaking team with deep Bordelais credentials. The 2022 vintage across Bordeaux was described as "dazzling" by critics, though the growing season was far from easy: frost, hail and prolonged hot, dry weather all tested the resolve of vignerons. What emerged from those conditions was fruit of unusual concentration and intensity.

## Viticulture

Selected vineyards across the Bordeaux appellation. The 2022 growing season reduced yields but concentrated flavours, resulting in deeply coloured wines with powerful tannins and surprising freshness.

## Winemaking

Temperature-controlled fermentation to preserve fruit purity. Oak ageing builds complexity and softens tannins. The wine is vinified for approachability: designed to drink well on release without needing years of cellaring.

## Tasting Notes

Intense ruby red colour. Aromas of fresh redcurrant, blackberry and a touch of flint. The wine has depth and texture on the palate with approachable tannins and a soft, succulent finish. It offers immediate pleasure while showing enough structure for medium-term development.

## Vintage 2022

Heat and drought are the markers of Bordeaux 2022, along with frost (nights of 3-4 April), hail and even forest fires in the Landiras. Four heatwaves brought temperatures up to 40°C, and the drought was described as "unprecedented." Yet the wines tell a different story. As Omri Ram of Château Lafleur explained, the dry conditions in early 2022 actually prepared the vines: "They self-regulated, growing smaller canopies. Unlike 2018, when the vines were fat and lazy, these vines were in training... they were marathon runners." The result broke expectations: deeply coloured wines with powerful tannins and, against all odds, a surprising degree of freshness. Jancis Robinson's team at the Wine Society described it simply: "hot and dry, high temperatures, low rainfall," yet producers who sorted thoroughly produced extraordinary wines.
