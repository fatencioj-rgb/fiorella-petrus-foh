## The Producer

Château d'Yquem is the sole Premier Cru Supérieur of the 1855 Classification. In a system that ranked five levels of red wine estates, only one sweet wine property was placed above all others, in a class entirely by itself. Two centuries later, that distinction remains unchallenged.

The estate sits on a hill overlooking the Sauternes appellation, 113 hectares of vineyard producing sometimes as little as one glass of wine per vine. The unique microclimate created by the Ciron river generates the morning mists essential for botrytis cinerea (noble rot), which concentrates sugars, acids and flavours to extraordinary levels. Multiple selective passes through the vineyard, sometimes over several weeks, harvest only perfectly botrytized grapes. In years that do not meet their standard, Yquem is simply not produced.

Fermentation and ageing take place in 100% new French oak barrels for approximately 20 months. The wines are among the longest-lived in the world: the oldest bottles from the 18th and 19th centuries remain drinkable and extraordinary today.

## Viticulture

The 113-hectare vineyard on the highest hill in Sauternes, planted predominantly to Sémillon with Sauvignon Blanc. The Ciron river creates morning mists for botrytis development. Multiple selective passes harvest only perfectly botrytized grapes. Production is tiny, reflecting an absolute refusal to compromise.

## Winemaking

Fermentation in 100% new French oak barrels. Approximately 20 months of barrel ageing. Each vintage is a testament to extreme patience and precision. In some years Yquem is not produced at all if quality standards cannot be met.

## Tasting Notes

After over 15 years in bottle, the 2008 shows extraordinary complexity. Golden amber colour. Aromas of dried apricot, orange marmalade, saffron, crème brûlée, exotic spices and beeswax. The palate is unctuous yet lifted by electric acidity, with layers of candied citrus, tropical fruit, honey and caramel. The finish extends seemingly without end.

## Vintage 2008

A challenging year across Bordeaux for red wines, but Sauternes excelled. The cool, humid autumn provided precisely what botrytis needs: morning mists from the Ciron river followed by afternoon warmth to prevent grey rot. The same cold, wet season that produced brilliant 2008 Champagne was a gift to Sauternes, where humidity is an asset, not a problem. The result was concentrated, elegant sweet wines with vibrant acidity that gives exceptional ageing potential. After over 15 years in bottle, the 2008 Yquem is now entering a magnificent drinking window.
