## The Producer

Quinta das Carvalhas is one of the most visually dramatic properties in the entire Douro Valley: a vast amphitheatre of terraced vineyards on the south-facing slopes overlooking Pinhão, the heart of Port country. It belongs to Real Companhia Velha, one of the oldest Port houses, founded in 1756 by Royal Charter of King José I to regulate and protect the Douro wine trade. That makes it not just old, but historically significant: this is the company that was created to define what Port is.

A 30 Year Old Tawny from this house carries the weight of that history. Three decades of patient oxidative ageing in small oak casks develops extraordinary depth: dried fig, walnut, toffee, orange marmalade, tobacco. The wine becomes silky, unctuous, and seemingly endless on the finish.

## Winemaking & Ageing

Fortified and then aged in small oak casks for an average of 30 years. Extended barrel ageing in the lodges develops extraordinary concentration, complexity and silky texture. Fractional blending ensures consistency across bottlings.

## Tasting Notes

Deep amber-mahogany colour. Complex bouquet of dried fig, walnut, toffee, orange marmalade, tobacco and exotic spices. Silky and unctuous on the palate, with extraordinary depth, layers of caramel, roasted nut and dried fruit, and a seemingly endless finish.
