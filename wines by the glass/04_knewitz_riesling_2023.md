## The Producer

The Knewitz family has been making wine in Appenheim since the 19th century, but it was the current generation, led by Tobias Knewitz, who transformed this modest estate into one of Rheinhessen's most exciting producers. Starting from just 5 hectares that his grandfather Gerold first estate-bottled in 1976, Tobias has grown the property to 15 hectares and earned VDP membership, Germany's most exclusive association of quality-focused estates.

What sets Knewitz apart is its location: the foot of the Westerberg, a prehistoric coral reef. This ancient geological formation gives an extraordinary diversity of soils in a small area: chalk, rock, marl and iron ore all within a few hundred metres of each other. Tobias describes his Rieslings as having a characteristic "Knewitz spiciness" that comes from this limestone and chalk, and it is this combination of ancient geology and modern ambition that makes the wines so compelling.

## Viticulture

This Erste Lage (premier cru equivalent) bottling is grown on limestone and chalk-rich soils at the foot of the Westerberg. The combination of cool westerly winds and warm exposure ensures a very long vegetation period, ideal for Riesling's aromatic development. All grapes are grown organically and hand-harvested at optimal ripeness.

## Winemaking

Vinification takes place in large, neutral oak barrels (traditional German Stückfass style), which allow slow, gentle fermentation without imparting overt oak flavour. This approach preserves varietal purity while adding subtle textural complexity. The wine is kept on its lees to build structure before bottling.

## Tasting Notes

Dry and crisp with a lively, racy acidity and juicy concentration. The nose presents grapefruit, Pippin apple and green herbal notes. On the palate, flavours gain power at mid-palate with sea salt and stony mineral details emerging towards a crisp, focused finish. The wine balances elegance with concentration, reflecting both the limestone terroir and the characteristic Knewitz spiciness.

## Vintage 2023

The 2023 growing season in Rheinhessen began with explosive springtime growth driven by warm temperatures and deep subsoil water reserves. Going into July, berry size and bunches appeared small, raising fears of another drought year. But then rain arrived, and the question became whether there would be too much. In the end, a very sunny and dry autumn (September and October) saved the vintage. For Riesling especially, the intense autumn sun allowed grapes in cooler sites and well-prepared vineyards to achieve remarkable concentration. Vinous described the 2023 Rheinhessen Rieslings as "bursting with flavour and delineated by freshness, but moderate in alcohol." The nationwide harvest volume (8.8 million hectolitres) was on par with the long-term average.
