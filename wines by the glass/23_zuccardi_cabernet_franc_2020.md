## The Producer

Familia Zuccardi has been named the World's Best Vineyard multiple times, and the reason is not what they grow but how they think. While Argentina is synonymous with Malbec, Sebastián Zuccardi has spent the last two decades asking a different question: what does each specific place want to be? In Paraje Altamira, at 1,100 metres in the Uco Valley, the answer turned out to be Cabernet Franc.

The "Apelación" series is Zuccardi's purest expression of terroir. Each wine is a single variety from a single place, and the Cabernet Franc from Paraje Altamira shows why this grape variety has found a second home in Argentina's high-altitude vineyards. The alluvial soils here are rich in calcium carbonate and large stones, creating wines of remarkable tension and mineral precision that feel closer to the Loire than to Bordeaux.

Their philosophy is direct: "Our challenge is to faithfully interpret the diversity of each place to express its identity in the wine." Grape movement by gravity, native yeasts, concrete vats, bottled unfiltered. Nothing added, nothing taken away.

## Viticulture

Planted at approximately 1,100 metres above sea level in Paraje Altamira, a high-altitude mountain desert. Complex alluvial soils rich in large stones, sand and calcium carbonate. Temperate climate with cold nights and significant diurnal temperature variation. The landscape is dominated by vineyards planted amidst large rocks, framed by the Cordón del Portillo and the Andean mountain range. Irrigated from the Tunuyán River via the historic Uco Canal.

## Winemaking

Grape movement by gravity. Fermentation in concrete vats with native yeasts. Maceration of 20 to 25 days. Aged in a combination of barrels and concrete vats. Bottled unfiltered to preserve the full expression of terroir.

## Tasting Notes

Deep, vibrant colour. The nose offers concentrated aromas of red and dark fruit with herbal complexity, earthy mineral notes and subtle spice. On the palate it is structured and precise, with fine-grained tannins, fresh acidity and a long, stony mineral finish that reflects the calcium-rich soils of Paraje Altamira.

## Vintage 2020

The Uco Valley's 2020 growing season benefited from the consistent conditions that high altitude provides: significant diurnal temperature variation (warm days, cold nights) that is the region's defining climatic feature. At 1,100 metres, Paraje Altamira's vineyards are above many of the weather extremes that affect lower-lying regions. The alluvial soils rich in calcium carbonate and large stones provide excellent drainage while retaining just enough moisture from the Tunuyán River irrigation. Zuccardi's gravity-flow winery and concrete-vat fermentation with native yeasts meant the character of this specific growing season was captured without manipulation.
