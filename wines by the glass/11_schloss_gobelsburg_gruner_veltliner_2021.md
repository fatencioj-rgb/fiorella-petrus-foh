## The Producer

Schloss Gobelsburg is the oldest winery in the Danube region. Its winemaking history dates back to 1171, when Cistercian monks established the estate, and their monastic principles of simplicity and strictness still guide the winemaking today. There is something powerful about a place that has been doing the same thing for over 850 years: it suggests that the terroir is speaking clearly enough to not need reinvention.

The estate maintains parcels in several Erste Lagen (premier cru equivalents), with each vineyard given its own distinct identity. Ried Lamm is dedicated exclusively to Grüner Veltliner and is, in character, the most Burgundian of their whites: broader, fuller, richer. This is because the Lamm vineyard sits in a particularly warm notch on the Heiligenstein hill, where deep loess soils ripen fruit exceptionally well. It is the estate's answer to the question of how far Grüner Veltliner can go in terms of weight and complexity without losing its identity.

## Viticulture

Ried Lamm is a single vineyard (cru) situated on the southeast slope of the Heiligenstein, one of Austria's most revered wine hills. The vineyard is south-facing and very well exposed, where erosion material from the Heiligenstein (perm, red siltstone) meets a high proportion of clay. The significant layer of loess (wind-blown soil) characterises this extremely warm site.

## Winemaking

Grapes are harvested by hand and vinified with minimal mechanical intervention. Fermentation uses mostly natural yeasts and takes place in large traditional oak casks or steel tanks. For the Ried Lamm, some contact with neutral oak adds subtle textural richness without overt wood flavour. The wine rests on its lees, developing the broader, fuller character that the warm Lamm vineyard naturally produces.

## Tasting Notes

The wine has a hint of nutty, creamy oak on the nose, alongside ripe pineapple, honeydew melon, apple and bergamot, with subtle savoury notes. On the palate there is a creaminess and a distinctive character of freshly peeled green banana expanding into woodland-scented freshness (moss, herbs). Good complexity, with extractive sweetness balanced by mineral salinity and well-integrated acidity. The finish is long and elegant, with touches of mango and orange peel in the aftertaste.

## Vintage 2021

In a departure from recent warm vintages, 2021 in Austria started with a chill. A relatively cold winter came with more snow than normal, and these cold, dry conditions held until spring. From mid-May onwards, repeated periods of ample rainfall led to vigorous budding and perfect flowering from mid-June to month's end. July and August brought unusual warmth with sporadic precipitation and some cooler days. The fall then dried out, allowing grapes to finally reach maturity in late October and November. Producers who remained patient were rewarded: quality fruit with acid levels and freshness not seen in years. For the Kamptal specifically, the cooler nights in the valley promised excellent balance between ripeness and vibrant acidity, with wines displaying longevity and vitality.
