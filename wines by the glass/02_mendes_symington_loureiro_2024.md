## The Producer

Mendes & Symington is a joint venture between two wine families with very different pedigrees that somehow make perfect sense together. Symington Family Estates has been making Port since 1882 and is responsible for Graham's, Dow's and Warre's, among others. Anselmo Mendes is widely considered the visionary of modern Vinho Verde, the winemaker who proved that this region could produce wines of seriousness and complexity, not just simple summer sippers.

Their partnership began in 2023 when the Symingtons acquired Casa de Rodas, a historic estate in the prestigious Monção and Melgaço sub-region, and brought Anselmo on as winemaker and vineyard consultant. They also acquired the Contacto brand, which Anselmo had built into one of Portugal's most successful wines internationally. This Loureiro is part of that Contacto range: a single-variety expression that showcases one of Portugal's most expressive and underrated white grapes.

## Viticulture

The grapes come from Quinta da Ferreira in the Ponte de Lima area, at approximately 200 metres above sea level on sloping, well-drained terrain about 25 kilometres inland from the Atlantic coast. The soils are granitic with a sandy-loam texture, featuring alluvial deposits and fluvial terraces that contribute minerality and complexity to the wine. The region benefits from a confluence of continental and oceanic influences, creating significant diurnal temperature variation that helps preserve natural acidity and aromatic intensity in the fruit. All grapes are hand-picked into small boxes and transported quickly to the winery to maintain freshness.

## Winemaking

Upon arrival the bunches are destemmed and gently pressed. The must is then clarified at extremely cold temperatures for 48 to 72 hours before fermentation begins. Fermentation takes place in stainless steel at a low, controlled temperature (12 to 16 °C) over a prolonged period to maximise aromatic expression. No oak is used; the wine is kept entirely in stainless steel to showcase varietal purity and terroir character.

## Tasting Notes

The wine presents a light, pale straw colour. On the nose it is expressive with lemon and citrus blossom, hints of white stone fruit (peach, nearing apricot), and subtle floral aromatics typical of Loureiro. The palate is elegant and light-bodied, with crisp, round acidity and a refreshing saline mineral undertone. The finish is long and vibrant, leaving a clean, citrus-driven impression.

## Vintage 2024

Portugal's 2024 harvest was of above-average quality with variable production by region, thanks to a mild, rainy year and relatively little instability during harvest. For Vinho Verde specifically, 2024 marked a return to more typical conditions after several years of erratic rainfall and prolonged heatwaves. Robert Parker described the 2024 Vinho Verdes as "superb," a vindication for this often-underestimated region. Sogrape's harvest report noted that production varied by area but quality was consistently good, with the mild year favouring aromatic white varieties like Loureiro.
