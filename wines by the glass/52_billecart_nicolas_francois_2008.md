## The Producer

Billecart-Salmon has been family-owned since 1818, making it one of the last major independent Champagne houses. Their philosophy has always been one of restraint and time. While much of the industry races to release wines young, Billecart pushes in the opposite direction: their non-vintage Brut guarantees at least three years on the lees, and their prestige cuvées wait far longer.

The Nicolas François Billecart, named after the house's founder, is their statement wine. The 2008 is perhaps the very last of the great 2008 Champagnes to reach the market, released 15 years after harvest. It spent over 150 months on its lees before being disgorged in the third quarter of 2022 with a dosage of just 2.9 g/l, barely perceptible. This is Champagne as an exercise in time and patience: the profound precision and molecular detail that define Billecart are transported to a higher level of fidelity by the legendary 2008 vintage.

## Viticulture

Grand Cru fruit from the finest classified villages: Chardonnay from Le Mesnil-sur-Oger, Chouilly and Cramant in the Côte des Blancs, Pinot Noir from Aÿ, Mareuil and Verzenay in the Montagne de Reims. These are among the most revered terroirs in Champagne, and the 60/40 Pinot-to-Chardonnay ratio gives the wine both structure and elegance.

## Winemaking

Partial vinification in traditional old oak casks (17%), with the remaining 83% in tanks. This oak element underpins the generous character without ever dominating. Over 12 years on the lees in Billecart's cellars, then disgorged with minimal dosage. The Magnum format allows for an even more graceful evolution during this extended ageing.

## Tasting Notes

Rich straw hue. The nose is layered and long: truffle, apricot, croissant, floral orange blossoms and marmalade. A saline chalk core erupts from its depths, framing pure lemon fruit and strawberry hull. It is emphatically, sublimely primary, as if time has only concentrated rather than evolved its character. With air, Pinot steps forward in gloriously pure red cherry fruits. A brilliant display of fine chalk minerality at its most profound.

## Vintage 2008

The 2008 growing season started inauspiciously: spring frosts were followed by hail, and July was cold and rainy. However, a frigid winter had rested the vines well, and conditions improved dramatically from August onward. The summer was dry with constant temperatures and normal rainfall, an absence of extremes that allowed slow, steady grape maturation. By September, hand-picking began on the 15th in ideal sunny conditions with healthy grapes showing a perfect sugar-acidity balance. The vintage is often compared to 1988 for its classical elegance and longevity. Krug nicknamed it "Classic Beauty." Bollinger's cellarmaster Gilles Descôtes later called it a "miracle year" given how badly it began. This bottling is its ultimate late expression.
