## The Producer

The Bouzereau family has been in Meursault for generations, but it is their quiet, unhurried approach that distinguishes them. Where some Burgundy domaines have embraced modernity and spectacle, Philippe Bouzereau practices simplicity, observation and vigilance. No more, no less. The philosophy is clear: intervene as little as possible, watch closely, and let the vineyard speak.

Les Grands Charrons is a sizeable lieu-dit situated just to the north of the line of Meursault premier crus, so close to premier cru status that many argue it deserves the promotion. Bouzereau holds around 1.5 hectares across five parcels, with vines planted in 1947, 1960, 1982 and 1992. It is this combination of old vines and a privileged site that gives the wine its beautiful concentration and precision, qualities that punch well above village-level expectations.

## Viticulture

The vineyard is on an eastern-exposed slope, with limestone at the top of the hill and stonier, eroded soil at the bottom. This variation across the five parcels contributes different facets to the blend: the limestone brings mineral definition while the stonier lower sections add depth and body. Average vine age in the older sections is 40 to 60 years.

## Winemaking

Grapes are harvested at full physiological maturity while preserving natural acidity. Pneumatic pressing is followed by fermentation using only indigenous (natural) yeasts. All wines are barrel-fermented and aged for 12 to 18 months in Burgundian pièces (228-litre barrels) and demi-muids, with an average of 20% new oak. The modest use of new wood allows the terroir and fruit to remain in focus.

## Tasting Notes

The wine presents a rich yet restrained aromatic profile. The bouquet is engaging, with hints of wild fennel, mint and citrus fruit alongside ripe yellow-fleshed stone fruit, a fresh citrus imprint and floral hints. On the palate there is impressive delineation, with a silver bead of acidity providing structure. The texture is delightfully rich and creamy, with layers of concentration leading to a fine, persistent finish.

## Vintage 2022

After a mild and dry winter, the 2022 vegetative cycle in Burgundy's Côte de Beaune began in early April. There were minor frost episodes in Saint-Aubin and Puligny-Montrachet, but nothing catastrophic. A spell of warm weather then accelerated growth, requiring intense vineyard work in pruning and de-budding. The summer was hot and dry. Drouhin described the resulting whites as offering "good maturity with particularly aromatic wines: citrus and ripe fruit mingle with delicate floral fragrances." Vinous titled their 2022 white Burgundy report "Made of Titanium," noting the structural density. Comparisons lean toward 2019 but fresher, and after the difficult, tiny 2021 vintage, the generous 2022 was a welcome relief for producers and buyers alike.
