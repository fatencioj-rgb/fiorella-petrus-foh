## The Producer

Graham's is one of the great names in Port, established in 1820 and now part of the Symington Family Estates, the family that also owns Dow's, Warre's and Cockburn's. If the Symingtons dominate Port today, it is because of houses like Graham's: known for a rich, concentrated, generous style that favours opulence over austerity.

The 10 Year Old Tawny is the entry point to their aged Tawny range, but "entry point" is relative. Ten years of slow oxidative ageing in small oak pipes in the lodges of Vila Nova de Gaia transforms what was once a dark, tannic ruby wine into something completely different: tawny in colour, nutty and smooth, with the rough edges polished away and replaced by caramel, dried fruit and spice.

## Winemaking & Ageing

After fortification, the wine ages in small oak casks (pipes) for an average of 10 years in the lodges of Vila Nova de Gaia. Extended oxidative ageing develops the characteristic tawny colour, nutty complexity and smooth texture. The final blend is assembled from wines of different ages to achieve the consistent house style.

## Tasting Notes

Tawny amber colour. Elegant nose of butterscotch, almond, dried fruit and subtle vanilla. Smooth and mellow on the palate with flavours of caramel, orange peel and walnut. Medium-sweet with a clean, lingering finish.
