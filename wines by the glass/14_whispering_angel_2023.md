## The Producer

Whispering Angel is the wine that changed how the world drinks rosé. Before Sacha Lichine created it in 2006, premium Provence rosé barely existed as a global category. Lichine, son of the legendary Bordeaux négociant Alexis Lichine, saw an opportunity to create a pale, dry, serious rosé that could command respect far beyond the beaches of the Côte d'Azur. He succeeded beyond anyone's expectations: Whispering Angel became a cultural phenomenon, synonymous with a certain lifestyle of effortless elegance.

The estate also produces several ultra-premium rosés (Garrus, Les Clans, Rock Angel) that prove Provence can match Burgundy in ambition and price. But Whispering Angel remains the heart of the operation: sourced from the most choice vineyards in the Esclans Valley and nearby areas, made in serious volumes without compromising on the freshness and quality that built its reputation.

The name comes from the chapel ceiling at Château d'Esclans, where carved angels appear to whisper to each other across the vaulted stone.

## Viticulture

Grapes are sourced from amongst the most choice vineyards in the vicinity of the Esclans Valley and nearby areas within the Côtes de Provence. Harvesting is conducted during the nighttime to avoid the heat of the day and to keep the crop fresh, preserving delicate aromatic compounds and natural acidity.

## Winemaking

Upon arrival at the winery, grapes are soft-crushed, then the temperature is reduced to enhance freshness and vivacity. Nitrogen pressing produces three grades of primarily free-run juice and ensures there is no oxidation. Depending on the grade of juice, fermentation takes place either in barrel or in stainless steel, with the majority in stainless steel at controlled temperatures. The wine is a blend of the best free-run and press juices. No malolactic fermentation, preserving the crisp, bone-dry character.

## Tasting Notes

Very pale in colour with the classic Provençal hue. The nose offers crisp berry and peach fruit with delicate floral nuances. On the palate the taste profile is full and lush while remaining bone dry, with a smooth, rounded texture and a mouthwatering finish. The wine delivers freshness and approachability in equal measure. The Magnum format allows for slower, more graceful evolution.

## Vintage 2023

Provence's 2023 was a vintage of excellent balance. After several warm years that pushed the region's wines toward richness, 2023 brought more moderate growing conditions that allowed for even ripening while maintaining the freshness and acidity essential for high-quality rosé. For Whispering Angel specifically, the nighttime harvesting and nitrogen pressing ensure that whatever conditions the vintage brings are captured at their freshest. The Magnum format (1.5L) adds another dimension: the larger volume-to-surface-area ratio means slower, more graceful evolution, finer bubbles and greater complexity than the same wine in a standard bottle.
