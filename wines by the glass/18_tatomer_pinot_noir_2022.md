## The Producer

Graham Tatomer is one of California's most compelling young winemakers, and the reason is what he chooses not to do. In a state where Pinot Noir often means ripe, extracted, oak-heavy wines pushing 14-15% alcohol, Tatomer goes in the opposite direction. He is drawn to the coldest sites, the lightest weights, and the kind of nuanced flavours that only emerge when a winemaker steps back and lets the vineyard lead.

His first love is actually Riesling (unusual for California), and that same cool-climate sensibility carries over to his Pinot Noir. The county cuvée blends fruit predominantly from Drum Canyon, with smaller portions from Kick-on Ranch and Sanford & Benedict, all located in and around the Sta. Rita Hills, some of the coldest, most wind-swept vineyard land in California. The result is a Pinot Noir that would feel at home in Burgundy or the Mosel Valley: light, precise, and quietly powerful.

## Viticulture

From among the coldest vineyard sites in Santa Barbara County, in and around the Sta. Rita Hills appellation. These coastal vineyards experience significant marine influence, producing grapes of lighter weight, nuanced flavours and bracing acidity. Multiple clones from multiple sites provide complexity in the final blend.

## Winemaking

Minimalist approach: letting fruit and vineyard character speak without heavy-handed intervention. Approximately 50% whole-cluster fermentation provides aromatic lift and palate feel. Natural yeast fermentation. Restrained oak ageing. Elegance comes from balance rather than extraction.

## Tasting Notes

The aromas are bright, pure and savoury, with struck flint, raspberry, dried cranberry, hibiscus and cinnamon on the nose. There is a quick snap of acidity on the palate, where subtle cherry, turned earth and sagebrush flavours align. The texture is velvety yet spry, finishing with subtly chalky tannins and excellent length.

## Vintage 2022

Santa Barbara County's 2022 vintage was marked by cool coastal conditions that define the Sta. Rita Hills. While much of California battled heat and drought in 2022, the marine influence of the Pacific (which funnels directly into the east-west oriented valleys of Santa Barbara) kept temperatures moderate and extended the growing season. For Tatomer's style of light-bodied, high-acid Pinot Noir, these conditions are ideal: enough warmth for phenolic ripeness without the high sugars that would push alcohol beyond his preferred 13%. The multiple vineyard sources (Drum Canyon, Kick-on Ranch, Sanford & Benedict) all benefited from these conditions, producing fruit of purity and definition.
