## The Producer

Late Bottled Vintage Port occupies a clever position in the Port world. It offers much of the character and depth of a vintage or even a declared Vintage Port, but without requiring decades of cellaring or the ritual of decanting. The wine is aged in oak for 4 to 6 years at the winery before bottling, meaning it arrives ready to drink.

Quinta da Vacaria is a property in the Douro Valley that produces this style with care and conviction. The traditional field blend of indigenous Portuguese grape varieties (Touriga Nacional, Touriga Franca, Tinta Roriz among them) is foot-trodden or mechanically trodden in lagares, and the fermentation is halted by the addition of grape spirit (aguardente) at approximately 77% vol., the moment that defines Port: capturing that natural sweetness and locking it in.

## Viticulture

Traditional field blend of indigenous Portuguese varieties planted on schist terraces along the steep hillsides of the Douro. The region's extreme continental climate with very hot, dry summers produces grapes of extraordinary concentration and intensity.

## Winemaking

Grapes are trodden in lagares. Fermentation halted by addition of grape spirit, retaining natural residual sugar. Aged in oak for 4 to 6 years before bottling (LBV style), combining depth with earlier accessibility.

## Tasting Notes

Deep ruby-purple colour. Rich aromas of dark berry fruit, plum, chocolate and spice. Full-bodied and concentrated on the palate, with firm but integrated tannins, sweet dark fruit and a long, warming finish.
