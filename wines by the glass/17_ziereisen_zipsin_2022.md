## The Producer

Hanspeter Ziereisen makes wines that should not exist in Germany. Located in Efringen-Kirchen, directly on the Rhine at the Dreiländereck ("three-country corner" of Germany, Alsace and Switzerland), his estate sits in a pocket of Baden so far south it has more in common with Burgundy than with the Mosel. And he knows it.

His ambition is clear and unapologetic: "We produce muscular wines. Not sumo wrestlers but decathletes: smooth, elegant, strong and athletic. Strength and balance is our target." Recipe winemaking is, in his words, "a horror." Individuality is the signature of Ziereisen.

The Jaspis series represents his top tier, named after the red jasper stone found in the vineyard soils. The Zipsin bottling comes from a specific plot on limestone, aged for approximately 2.5 years in neutral oak, and bottled unfined and unfiltered. It is among the most serious Pinot Noirs made in Germany, from a region that remains surprisingly under-recognised despite its exceptional quality.

## Viticulture

Vineyards on limestone soils in the southernmost part of Baden. Sustainable viticulture. The warm microclimate of the Dreiländereck allows full phenolic ripeness in Pinot Noir, something that much of Germany cannot consistently achieve.

## Winemaking

Hand-harvested grapes undergo extended maceration to build structure and depth. Fermentation uses indigenous yeasts. The wine is aged in neutral oak barrels for approximately 2.5 years, allowing slow development of complexity without overt oak influence. Bottled unfined and unfiltered, as is standard for all Jaspis wines.

## Tasting Notes

Deep in colour, ripe and seductive on the elegant nose, displaying perfectly ripe cherry and dark berry aromas intertwined with meaty, floral and delicate earthy nuances. On the palate the wine is structured and powerful yet refined, with flavours of blackberry, plum and stony minerality backed by dusty tannins and good acidity that lends freshness. The finish is long and complex.

## Vintage 2022

The 2022 vintage in southern Baden brought warm, dry conditions ideal for Pinot Noir, continuing a trend of increasingly ripe vintages in this part of Germany. For the Dreiländereck (at the Swiss-French-German border), where temperatures consistently allow full phenolic ripeness, 2022 delivered concentrated fruit without the issues of drought stress seen further north. Ziereisen's limestone soils retained moisture well, and the extended 2.5-year barrel ageing further integrated the vintage's natural power. The resulting wines show excellent depth while retaining the freshness and structure characteristic of this terroir.
