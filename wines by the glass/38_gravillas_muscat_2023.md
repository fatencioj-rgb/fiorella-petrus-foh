## The Producer

Clos du Gravillas is a micro estate in every sense: just 5 hectares, 1,200 cases a year, and two people, Nicole and John Bojanowski, doing everything themselves. They are perched on the high hillsides of Saint-Jean-de-Minervois, a tiny village in the Parc Naturel du Haut Languedoc that is famous for one thing: the ripe, pure bottling of the Muscat grape in its fortified sweet form.

The name "Douce Providence" (sweet providence) captures both the style and the philosophy. This is a wine that relies on the natural generosity of its terroir: very stony limestone soils at high elevation, where gradual ripening produces grapes of remarkable concentration and freshness. The fortification is gentle, barely enough to halt fermentation, resulting in a wine that is sweet without being heavy, fortified without being hot. It is what Muscat tastes like when everything is done with care and nothing is rushed.

## Viticulture

Very stony limestone soils at high elevation in Saint-Jean-de-Minervois. The geological and climatic environment promotes gradual ripening and a naturally taut balance. Hand-harvested with meticulous sorting. Yield management at 25 hl/ha.

## Winemaking

Lightly fortified (mutage) to halt fermentation at the appellation minimum of 125 g/l residual sugar. This gentle fortification preserves the fresh, aromatic Muscat character while retaining natural sweetness. The result is lower in alcohol than most VDN styles, keeping the wine vibrant and drinkable.

## Tasting Notes

Rich and flattering floral nose with lots of mineral freshness and bright colour. The palate is smooth and very voluptuous, filled with pineapple and tropical fruit, leading into a refreshing finish. Weight and depth in spades, yet never heavy or cloying.

## Vintage 2023

Good conditions for Muscat production in the limestone hills. The elevated terroir and stony soils provided the gradual ripening needed for concentrated yet balanced sweet wines.
