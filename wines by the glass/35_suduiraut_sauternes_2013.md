## The Producer

If Yquem is the king of Sauternes, Suduiraut is the queen: almost as old, almost as grand, and in many vintages nearly as good. The estate is a Premier Cru Classé of 1855, and its park-like grounds, with stunning gardens and lakes designed by Le Nôtre (the architect of Versailles), make it one of the most beautiful properties in all of Bordeaux.

The property dates back to the 16th century, but it was the Count who commissioned Le Nôtre's gardens who elevated it to something truly special. Today Suduiraut consistently produces some of Sauternes' most opulent, generous wines, with a richness and unction that distinguish them from the more linear, citrus-driven style of Yquem. Where Yquem is about precision and eternity, Suduiraut is about seduction and indulgence.

## Viticulture

The vineyard is planted predominantly to Sémillon with some Sauvignon Blanc on classic Sauternes terroir. The Ciron river generates morning mists essential for botrytis cinerea development. Multiple selective passes through the vineyard harvest only botrytis-affected grapes at optimal concentration.

## Winemaking

Fermentation in oak barrels. Extended barrel ageing builds richness and complexity. The 2013 carries approximately 150 g/l of residual sugar, balanced by tight acidity that gives structure and longevity. Full, rich and powerful, built for long-term ageing.

## Tasting Notes

Deep, pure and refined on the nose, with aromas of exotic fruits, hazelnut and honey complicated by smoky botrytis. Opulent on the attack, then large-scaled and solidly structured in the middle palate. Very pure, precise flavours of caramel apple, mango, papaya, grapefruit and honey. Fresh and zesty with an almost minty-herbal edge. Deceptively rich: the acidity is present but wrapped in fruit, leading to a long, linear finish.

## Vintage 2013

Sauternes excelled in 2013, a year that was otherwise mixed across Bordeaux for dry reds. The key for sweet wines came from mid-September onwards, when alternate wet and dry spells led to the emergence of botrytis cinerea on very fine, ripe, healthy grapes. Wine Enthusiast described the result as "full and rich, a powerful wine packed with botrytis-driven flavours of wild honey and peach, balanced by tight acidity." The 150 g/l residual sugar is held in check by that acidity, making this a Sauternes built for long-term ageing rather than immediate gratification.
