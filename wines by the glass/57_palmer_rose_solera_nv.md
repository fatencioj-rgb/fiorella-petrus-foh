## The Producer

Palmer & Co was founded in 1947 by seven grower families in Avize who decided, in the shadow of the Second World War, that pooling their Grand Cru and Premier Cru vineyards would produce better wine than going it alone. They moved to Reims in 1959, acquired cellars, and built what is today a cooperative of over 200 members farming across 40 crus in the Montagne de Reims and Côte des Blancs.

What separates Palmer from other cooperatives is the seriousness of the winemaking. Under chef de cave Xavier Berdin, the house ages its wines far longer than the appellation demands and uses old Burgundian oak casks and perpetual reserves to build complexity. The Rosé Solera is perhaps the most distinctive wine in their range: a rosé Champagne whose colour and depth come not from simple blending or saignée, but from a solera of Pinot Noir that has been running continuously for over forty years. It is a technique borrowed from Jerez, applied to Champagne, producing something with a memory that stretches back decades.

## Viticulture

The fruit is sourced from Premier Cru and Grand Cru vineyards across the Montagne de Reims, where the chalk soils and northern exposures give Pinot Noir its characteristic structure and depth. Chardonnay comes from classified parcels in the Côte des Blancs, contributing freshness and precision to the blend.

## Winemaking

Traditional method with secondary fermentation in bottle. The defining element here is the solera: 8% of the final blend comes from a perpetual reserve of Pinot Noir started over forty years ago and maintained in stainless steel. Each year, a portion is drawn off for blending and replenished with fresh wine, meaning the solera carries a thread of every vintage since its inception. The remainder of the blend undergoes partial fermentation in 15-year-old Burgundian oak barrels for eight to ten months before assemblage. Reserve wines make up 30–35% of the blend. The wine is aged on the lees for three years in standard bottles and five years in Magnum. Dosage is approximately 8 g/l.

## Tasting Notes

A vibrant, pale rose-gold in the glass with a fine, persistent bead. The nose is intense and layered: wild strawberry leads, followed by red and black currants, delicate vanilla, cinnamon spice, and a pastry-like richness from the extended lees ageing. On the palate it is fresh yet full-bodied, with a soft creaminess and fine tannins from the solera Pinot Noir. Red fruit persists through the mid-palate, joined by notes of lychee and a subtle ginger warmth on the finish. The mousse is refined and the length impressive. It is a rosé Champagne with genuine depth and memory.
