## The Producer

Pedro Ximénez sherry is one of the most extreme wines in the world. It begins not in a winery but on a rooftop: after harvest, the PX grapes are laid out in the scorching Andalusian sun until they become practically raisins, a process called asoleo. What you are tasting in the glass started as dried fruit on straw mats.

Bodegas Emilio Hidalgo is a family-owned house in the heart of Jerez de la Frontera, producing sherry since the 19th century. Their PX ages in the traditional Criaderas y Solera system, a fractional blending method where younger wines are periodically introduced into barrels containing older wines, meaning the solera contains a thread of the oldest wine in its history. No individual bottle has a vintage because every bottle contains a blend of ages stretching back decades.

The result is a wine of almost impossible density and concentration: ebony-coloured, thick enough to coat the glass, sweet enough to pour over ice cream, yet balanced by the oxidative complexity that only decades in porous oak can provide.

## Viticulture

Pedro Ximénez grapes grown on limestone-rich albariza, clay-dominant barros and sandy arenas soils in Jerez. Sun-dried (asoleo) immediately after harvest until practically raisins, concentrating sugars to extraordinary levels.

## Winemaking

After sun-drying, fermentation starts slowly from the dense, sweet grape must and is halted by fortification to maintain all natural sugars. The wine then ages in the traditional Criaderas y Solera system, exposed to controlled oxidation in porous oak barrels over many years.

## Tasting Notes

Ebony in colour with iodine highlights. Incredibly dense and concentrated. Aromas of figs, raisins, dates and molasses. Enormously sweet, velvety and soft on the palate, yet well balanced. The viscous texture coats the mouth with flavours of dried fruit, dark caramel and bittersweet chocolate. The finish is very long.

## Ageing

Non-vintage, produced through the solera fractional blending system which combines wines of multiple ages, ensuring consistency and layered complexity in every bottling.
