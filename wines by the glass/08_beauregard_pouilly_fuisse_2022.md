## The Producer

Frédéric Burrier and Château de Beauregard represent the new guard of Pouilly-Fuissé, an appellation that has fought hard to shed its reputation as "lesser Burgundy." With approximately 19 hectares across the appellation and a portfolio that now includes premier cru sites (awarded in 2020), Beauregard is making the case that the Mâconnais can produce white Burgundy of genuine complexity and ageability.

2022 is a milestone vintage: it is the first year the domaine is certified organic. This decision reflects a broader philosophical shift toward expressing terroir without shortcuts. The flagship cuvée draws from 12 of the estate's 19 hectares, blending fruit from different communes to create a complete expression of what Pouilly-Fuissé can be at its best.

## Viticulture

The vineyards span varied terroirs including clay-limestone slopes and marl across the Pouilly-Fuissé appellation, with different communes contributing distinct characters to the final blend. Now certified organic, with practices designed to preserve soil health and biodiversity.

## Winemaking

Fermentation is split between traditional 228-litre Burgundian barrels (approximately 50%) and tile-lined concrete vats (50%), a combination designed to balance richness with freshness. The wine is kept on its fine lees for 10 months, then the assemblage is further aged in stainless steel for an additional six months after blending. This extended élevage adds complexity and depth without excessive oak influence.

## Tasting Notes

The nose is attractively balanced between fresh, fruity aromatics and creamy opulence. Aromas of yellow plum, lemon verbena and light lanolin are present alongside a generous warmth. On the palate the texture is rich and creamy, yet well-balanced with a fine mineral framework and good tension. Flavours of ripe citrus, white peach and subtle almond lead to a long, satisfying finish.

## Vintage 2022

The same conditions as described for the Bouzereau above applied across the Mâconnais, though the southern location generally means warmer conditions. After a mild, dry winter and early budburst in April, the summer was hot and dry. For Beauregard specifically, this is the first vintage produced under certified organic status, a milestone that coincided with a year where healthy grapes were relatively easy to achieve due to the dry conditions (less disease pressure). The generous 2022 provided impressive ripeness, and the combination of tile-lined vats (50%) and barrel fermentation (50%) allowed Burrier to capture both the opulence of the year and enough freshness to avoid heaviness.
