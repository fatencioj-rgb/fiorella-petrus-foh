## The Producer

Gérard Bertrand's "Légend Vintage" collection is something extraordinary: single-vintage Vin Doux Naturel wines that have been ageing in oxidative conditions for decades, sometimes close to a century. The 1976 has spent nearly 50 years developing the complex rancio character that defines tuilé-style Maury.

Maury is a Vin Doux Naturel appellation in the Roussillon, where grapes are fortified on the whole grains (mutage sur grains), meaning grape spirit is added while the skins are still in contact with the juice. This technique gives the wines more colour, tannin and structure than other VDN methods. What happens next is the real story: the wine is then left to age in oxidative conditions for years, decades, or in this case, nearly half a century. Oxygen slowly transforms the wine, developing the flavours the French call rancio: a savoury, nutty, caramelised complexity that cannot be achieved any other way.

These are wines to discover today or in 100 years. Time is essentially irrelevant to them.

## Viticulture

Schist soils in the Roussillon. Hot, dry Mediterranean climate producing grapes of extraordinary ripeness and concentration. The 1976 vintage grapes were grown in a pre-industrial era of Roussillon viticulture.

## Winemaking

Fortified on the whole grains (mutage sur grains) during fermentation. Then aged for nearly 50 years in oxidative conditions (tuilé style). The decades of controlled oxidation in barrel develop the complex rancio character: nuts, dried fruits, caramel, coffee, beeswax.

## Tasting Notes

Loaded with rich nut and rancio flavours. Aromas of dried orange peel, gingerbread, toasted macadamia nut, molasses, cocoa, coffee and caramel. Flavours of dried red plum, fig, honey, walnuts, beeswax and soft white pepper. Complex, spicy and full, with an endless finish that is smooth and lingering.

## Vintage 1976

A single-vintage wine that has spent nearly 50 years in oxidative ageing. The extraordinary concentration and complexity are the product of half a century of patient maturation.
