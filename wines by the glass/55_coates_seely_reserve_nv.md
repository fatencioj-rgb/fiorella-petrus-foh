## The Producer

Coates & Seely was born from a consoling bottle of Champagne and a lost rugby match. In 2007, on the eve of the Rugby World Cup final, Nicholas Coates travelled to Bordeaux to support his old friend Christian Seely, who was living in a country of 60 million people all wanting England to lose. England did lose. Halfway through the second consoling bottle of Champagne, Coates said: "We live in Hampshire, where we have chalk slopes. Have you ever thought of making English sparkling wine?"

The idea stuck. Nick Coates, a retired City financier, and Christian Seely, the managing director of AXA Millésimes' wine division (responsible for Château Pichon-Longueville, Château Suduiraut and Quinta do Noval) brought together English ambition and world-class winemaking experience. They planted their Hampshire chalk slopes with Chardonnay, Pinot Noir and Pinot Meunier, the same varieties, and geologically identical chalk, as Champagne.

They call their method "méthode Britannique" with a wink. Production is deliberately small-scale and artisanal (around 45,000 bottles a year), allowing them to make wines to the most exacting standards in their own unique way.

## Viticulture

The vineyards sit on chalk soils in Hampshire that are geologically identical to those of the Champagne region. This chalk provides the mineral backbone, excellent drainage and high natural acidity that are essential for producing world-class sparkling wine. The cool English climate means a long growing season with gradual ripening.

## Winemaking

Traditional method with secondary fermentation in bottle. The Brut Réserve is a blend of 65% Chardonnay and 35% Pinot Noir, incorporating reserves from previous years for depth and consistency. Lees-aged for 2.5 to 3 years, building the creamy texture and biscuity complexity that characterise the best traditional method wines.

## Tasting Notes

Richness with notes of apples, elderflower and hedgerow fruits. The palate is lean and structured, with high acidity, sweet-and-sour sensations and a fine chalky minerality. Long, succulent and brisk on the finish. A wine that is unmistakably English in its freshness, yet entirely serious in its ambition.
