## The Producer

Château de Moncets is one of those Bordeaux properties that quietly goes about making excellent wine without the fanfare of its famous neighbours. The estate sits in the commune of Néac, at the crossroads of Saint-Émilion, Montagne-Saint-Émilion and Pomerol, prestigious company that speaks to the quality of the terroir. The property's history dates back to 1763 when Antoine Roussillon, known as "the prince of grape varieties," settled at this already renowned estate.

While Moncets is primarily known as a Lalande de Pomerol red wine producer, this Bordeaux Blanc represents something different: a pioneering style focused on balancing freshness with the inherent power of Bordeaux varieties. The domaine is certified organic (AB label) and combines tradition with modern innovation, making it a property to watch in a region where white wines are increasingly taken seriously.

## Viticulture

The vineyard surrounds a beautiful 19th-century residence and shaded park. Soils are typical of the Right Bank: clay-limestone and gravel deposits providing excellent drainage. Certified organic, with sustainable practices throughout.

## Winemaking

Pneumatic pressing is used to extract clean, high-quality juice. Fermentation is temperature-controlled, with the wine seeing a combination of stainless steel and oak depending on the vintage. The goal is a harmonious balance between fruit expression and textural complexity, freshness without austerity.

## Tasting Notes

The wine opens with fresh aromas of white and exotic fruits (pear, quince, hints of pineapple) combined with delicate floral notes and a subtle herbaceous character typical of Sauvignon Blanc. On the palate it is lively and crisp, with good volume in the mid-palate and a refreshing, fruity finish driven by citrus and mineral undertones. The Sémillon component adds roundness and body.

## Vintage 2023

2023 in Bordeaux turned out to be the second-hottest vintage on record after 2022, with temperatures above the 30-year average in every month except February and July. Winter was cold and wet, ensuring vines had good water reserves from the start. Budburst came in mid-to-late March (early), raising frost risks that largely did not materialise. For the dry whites, harvest started in the second half of August and finished in early September, capturing grapes with good aromatic intensity. Overall, 2023 Bordeaux wines are lower in alcohol than previous vintages (often by 1%), yet show no green flavours. The whites are aromatically complex, soft and silky, with a vibrance that distinguishes them.
