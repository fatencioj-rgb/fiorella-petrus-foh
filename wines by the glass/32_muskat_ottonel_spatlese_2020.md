## The Producer

The Neusiedlersee region is Austria's sweet wine paradise. Located on the eastern shore of Lake Neusiedl, Central Europe's largest steppe lake, it is one of the few places in Europe where noble rot (botrytis cinerea) can be relied upon almost every autumn. The lake's shallow, warm waters generate misty mornings that settle over the surrounding vineyards, creating perfect conditions for the fungus to develop and concentrate the grapes.

Muskat Ottonel is one of the most aromatically expressive grape varieties used for sweet wine here. Unlike the heavier, richer styles made from Welschriesling or Chardonnay, Muskat Ottonel brings a floral elegance and perfumed character that makes for sweet wines of delicacy rather than density. It is the prettier, more fragrant side of Neusiedlersee.

## Viticulture

Vineyards on the eastern shore of Lake Neusiedl. The Pannonian climate is characterised by hot, dry summers and cold winters. The lake's influence generates the autumn mists essential for noble rot development and natural concentration in the grapes.

## Winemaking

Late-harvested grapes with naturally concentrated sugars are gently pressed and fermented at cool temperatures in stainless steel to preserve the delicate Muskat aromatics. Fermentation is stopped at the desired sweetness level, retaining the grape's natural floral character.

## Tasting Notes

Intensely aromatic with floral notes of elderflower and rose, exotic fruit (lychee, mango), and honeyed sweetness balanced by refreshing acidity. The palate is elegant and perfumed, with a silky texture and clean finish.
