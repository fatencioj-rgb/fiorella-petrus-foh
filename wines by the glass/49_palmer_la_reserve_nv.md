## The Producer

Palmer & Co is a house built on the idea of patience. While many Champagne producers rush their wines to market, Palmer ages its non-vintage Brut for four years on the lees in standard bottles and six years in Magnum, three times what the appellation requires. That commitment to time defines everything about the house.

What makes La Réserve particularly interesting is its perpetual reserve system. The reserve wines used in each blend are composed of previous blends of La Réserve itself, meaning each bottle carries a thread of DNA from every vintage that came before it. It is a wine that literally contains its own history. Half the grapes come from Grand Cru and Premier Cru vineyards, and up to 35% of the final blend is reserve wine, giving a depth and maturity that belies its accessible positioning.

The Magnum format is not just a larger bottle here. The slower, more graceful ageing that occurs in Magnum means the wine develops differently: more complexity, finer bubbles, greater harmony.

## Viticulture

Grapes are sourced from Grand Cru and Premier Cru vineyards across the Montagne de Reims and the Côte des Blancs. The Chardonnay-dominant blend draws its precision and freshness from the chalk soils of these classified villages, while Pinot Noir from the Montagne contributes structure and Meunier adds generosity and fruit.

## Winemaking

Traditional method with secondary fermentation in bottle. The key distinction is the extended lees ageing: six years in Magnum. The liqueur d'expédition is uniquely solera-based, adding yet another layer of complexity. Dosage is approximately 6.9 g/l, placing it firmly in Brut territory while retaining a subtle roundness.

## Tasting Notes

Fresh, bracing acidity with extremely fine and impressively vigorous bubbles. The nose reveals apples, elderflower and hedgerow fruits, with an underlying richness from the extended ageing. On the palate there is rare fullness and maturity: the grace and power of Pinot Noir, the generosity of Meunier, and the precision of Chardonnay all in balance. The finish is long and satisfying, with a creamy, biscuity depth.
