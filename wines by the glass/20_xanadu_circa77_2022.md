## The Producer

Xanadu has been in Margaret River since 1977, and the "Circa 77" name honours that founding year. But the real story is what happened more recently: in a competition featuring over 12,000 entries from around the world at the International Wine & Spirit Competition, a Xanadu Cabernet was selected as the number one red wine in the world. For an estate that many still consider "under the radar," this was a statement moment.

Under winemaker Glenn Goodall's leadership since 2006, the approach is traditional but precise: hand-harvesting, wild fermentation, sustainable farming, and an unshakeable belief that Margaret River is one of the great Cabernet Sauvignon regions on earth. The Circa 77 range embodies their love for this place, reflecting the beauty of the region through structured, elegant wines that speak of their gravelly terroir.

## Viticulture

Relatively flat vineyards rooted in free-draining deep, gravelly loam soil over clay. The original varieties planted in 1977 include Cabernet Sauvignon and Sémillon. Margaret River's maritime climate provides consistent conditions: warm enough to ripen Cabernet fully, cool enough to preserve structure and freshness.

## Winemaking

Hand-harvested grapes undergo wild (indigenous) fermentation to allow full terroir expression. The wine is matured in French oak barrels with precise selection of cooperages to build structure while respecting the varietal character. The approach favours elegance and regional typicity over excessive extraction.

## Tasting Notes

Plump cherry, blackcurrant, pencil lead, dried savoury herbs and a toasty, chocolaty oak aroma. The palate is full-figured but with freshness and structure, wound tightly by savoury, slightly drying tannins. Dark fruits of the forest, mulberries, and cassis intermingle with hints of graphite on the long finish.

## Vintage 2022

Margaret River's 2022 was a warmer-than-average vintage. Xanadu described it as producing wines with "excellent fruit concentration and structure." The maritime influence of the Indian Ocean, which defines Margaret River's climate by keeping temperatures moderated, remained the saving grace even in a warm year. The region's gravelly loam soils retain enough moisture to avoid vine stress. For context, it was in this competitive vintage that Xanadu's Cabernet achieved its extraordinary IWSC recognition, suggesting the warmth in 2022 was not excessive but rather ideal for full Cabernet Sauvignon ripeness in this maritime-influenced region.
