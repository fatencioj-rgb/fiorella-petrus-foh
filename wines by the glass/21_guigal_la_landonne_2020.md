## The Producer

La Landonne is one third of the most legendary trilogy in the Rhône Valley. Together with La Mouline and La Turque, the "La La" wines of Guigal have defined what Côte-Rôtie can be at its most extreme and ambitious. La Mouline debuted in 1966, La Landonne in 1978, and each wine represents a different facet of the appellation's terroir.

What makes La Landonne unique within the trilogy is its origin on the Côte Brune: iron-rich schist and mica soils that produce wines of exceptional power, depth and tannic structure, the darkest and most muscular of the three. The vineyard itself is almost impossibly steep, a 45-degree slope that requires harvesters to start picking from the bottom and work their way up, placing grape bunches in bins set on sleds that are dragged up the hill.

Guigal vinifies an astonishing 40% of all wine produced in the entire Côte-Rôtie appellation. Yet La Landonne remains tiny, rare, and almost mythically concentrated. The 42 months of ageing in new French oak (3.5 years) would overwhelm a lesser wine, but here the oak is absorbed into the structure as if it were always there.

## Viticulture

One of the steepest vineyards of the Côte Brune, on a 45-degree slope. Iron-rich schist and mica soils. These dark, heavy soils produce wines of exceptional power compared to the lighter, more elegant wines of the Côte Blonde across the appellation.

## Winemaking

Minimal destemming. Extended maceration to extract maximum colour, tannin and flavour. Aged for 42 months (3.5 years) in new French oak barrels. This prolonged élevage integrates the oak seamlessly into the wine's powerful structure. The result is a wine of exceptional concentration, complexity, and decades-long ageing potential.

## Tasting Notes

The wine explodes from the glass with smoky blackberry jam, menthol, crushed rocks, cured meat, iodine, licorice, cloves and black olive tapenade. On the palate it is full-bodied and concentrated, with building tannins and incredible structure. The wine possesses riveting depth and brings together all the finest elements of the Guigal house style.

## Vintage 2020

Drought and heat were the main concerns throughout the growing season in the Northern Rhône. Despite this, an early harvest led to surprisingly fresh wines. For Côte-Rôtie specifically, the conditions were ideal for Syrah on the steep schist slopes, which retained just enough moisture to avoid shutdown. The vintage produced wines of extraordinary concentration but also unexpected freshness, a combination that critics found remarkable. Robert Parker's reviewer awarded the 2020 La Landonne a potentially perfect score. The recommendation: hide bottles for at least a decade, then enjoy over 50 years of drinking life (2035-2075).
