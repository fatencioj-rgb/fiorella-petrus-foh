## The Producer

H.M. Borges is a family-owned Madeira house established in 1877, one of the smaller producers on the island who maintain a traditional, artisanal approach. Malvasia (known internationally as Malmsey) is the richest and sweetest of Madeira's four noble grape varieties, and it is the style that first made the island famous centuries ago. Shakespeare wrote about Malmsey; the Duke of Clarence allegedly drowned in a butt of it.

At 20 years old, this Malvasia has spent two decades developing in the canteiro system: natural warming in the attic lofts of the Borges lodges, where the subtropical Madeiran heat slowly transforms the wine. The combination of extreme sweetness from the Malvasia grape and extreme acidity from Madeira's volcanic terroir creates a tension that few other wines in the world can match. It should be overwhelming, but instead it is harmonious: lusciously sweet yet never heavy.

## Viticulture

Malvasia grown on the island's lower, warmer south-facing slopes on volcanic soils. The grape variety requires warmth to ripen fully, and Madeira's south coast provides it with a subtropical generosity.

## Winemaking & Ageing

Fortified during fermentation to retain natural sweetness. Aged for 20 years using the canteiro method (natural warming in attic lofts). Extended oxidative ageing in seasoned oak casks develops extraordinary complexity while Madeira's signature high acidity preserves freshness.

## Tasting Notes

Deep amber-mahogany colour. Rich, complex nose of burnt caramel, dried fig, orange peel, dark chocolate, coffee and exotic spices. Lusciously sweet on the palate but never heavy, thanks to Madeira's trademark searing acidity. The finish is endless, with flavours of toffee, roasted nuts and candied fruit lingering beautifully.
