## The Producer

Château Minuty, near Saint-Tropez, is one of the names that defined what modern Provence rosé could be. For decades, the estate has been associated with precise, fresh, elegant wines that highlight purity and finesse. In 2023 LVMH acquired the property, a signal of just how seriously the luxury world takes Provençal rosé.

What makes Minuty remarkable is their relationship with Grenache. In the last decade of the 20th century, the family realised that the greatest plots in their vineyard were planted to old Grenache vines. When handled with overwhelming gentleness, Grenache can produce wines of aromatic complexity as well as deep flavours and body without tannin or aggressiveness. This insight transformed their winemaking and remains the foundation of the M de Minuty cuvée.

M de Minuty is the estate's most recognisable wine: a multi-terroir blend built to express the diversity of the entire Côtes de Provence appellation rather than a single site. It is also one of the few estates in Provence where harvesting is still done entirely by hand.

## Viticulture

The cuvée is built from a rigorous selection of grapes from the best terroirs across the Côtes de Provence appellation. The diversity of soils (schist, limestone, clay) and exposures contributes complexity to the final blend. All fruit is hand-harvested.

## Winemaking

The grapes are cold-pressed (direct pressing) with extreme care to extract only the palest, most delicate free-run juice. Fermentation takes place at cool, controlled temperatures in stainless steel. No malolactic fermentation is carried out, maintaining crisp, vibrant acidity. The wine is kept entirely in stainless steel throughout its élevage, capturing the fresh and contemporary spirit of Provence rosé.

## Tasting Notes

Very pale, luminous pink colour. On the nose, aromas of crisp red fruits emerge, followed by delicate peach and subtle floral notes. The palate seduces with its liveliness and volume, delivering a wonderfully indulgent sensation while remaining bone dry. Light, bright, fresh and smooth, with a clean, refreshing finish.

## Vintage 2025

Wine Spectator's 2025 Provence report notes that, with better water reserves and less heat than prior years, the vintage shows higher acidity levels than recent warm years. Wines from top terroirs made by meticulous winegrowers successfully match that tension with ample concentration and texture. For Minuty, which hand-harvests and cold-presses with extreme care, these conditions are ideal: the freshness of the vintage aligns perfectly with their house style of pale, crisp, bone-dry rosé.
