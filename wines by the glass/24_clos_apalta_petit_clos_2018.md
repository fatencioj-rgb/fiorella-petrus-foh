## The Producer

Clos Apalta tells one of wine's great stories of rediscovery. When the Marnier-Lapostolle family (of Grand Marnier fame) acquired this hillside property in Chile's Apalta Valley, the 30 hectares were already planted with vines of Cabernet Sauvignon and Carménère dating back to 1915. For decades these old vines had been quietly producing fruit of extraordinary character, waiting for someone to recognise their potential.

The Grand Vin, Clos Apalta, quickly became one of South America's most celebrated wines. Le Petit Clos was created in 2014 as a different interpretation of the same terroir: using the mastery of blending that is the heart of the domaine's philosophy, but in a more immediate, accessible style. The estate is certified organic and biodynamic.

What makes Clos Apalta unusual is the centrality of Carménère. While Bordeaux abandoned this grape variety after phylloxera, it survived in Chile (long misidentified as Merlot) and here, on century-old vines, it reaches an expression found nowhere else: earthy, stony, deeply complex.

## Viticulture

Vineyards in the Apalta Valley, Colchagua, with vines dating back to 1915. Granitic hillside soils. Certified organic and biodynamic. The unique microclimate of Apalta produces wines of great concentration and earthy complexity.

## Winemaking

Fermentation followed by ageing in French oak barrels, initially in new oak, then continuing in a combination of new and seasoned barrels. The blend is assembled to express the signature Apalta character of earthiness and power while remaining more immediately accessible than the Grand Vin.

## Tasting Notes

Ripe and powerful with the Apalta signature: earthy and stony. The nose displays deep dark fruit, spice and subtle oak influence. On the palate the wine shows excellent concentration, with rich layers of dark berry, cassis and herbal complexity, supported by firm but polished tannins. The finish is long, with mineral and earthy undertones.

## Vintage 2018

In Colchagua's Apalta Valley, the 2018 vintage delivered excellent conditions for both Carménère and Cabernet Sauvignon. The Clos Apalta Grand Vin from the same year uses 64% Carménère (with 18% Cabernet Sauvignon and 18% Merlot), reaching 15% alcohol, indicating the warmth and ripeness of the vintage. Le Petit Clos, as the second wine with its own distinct blend (53% Carménère, 32% Cabernet Sauvignon), captures the Apalta signature at a more accessible level. The century-old vines and organic/biodynamic practices gave the fruit a complexity and depth that younger plantings simply cannot replicate.
