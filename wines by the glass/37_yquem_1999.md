## The Producer

Same estate as above. But the 1999 adds 9 more years of bottle age to the equation, and with Yquem, time does not diminish the wine; it transforms it. At 27 years old, this is a wine that has had a quarter-century to develop tertiary complexity: the fresh tropical fruit of youth giving way to caramel, dried flowers, tobacco and marmalade. It is Yquem at the point where it stops being about the vintage and starts being about eternity.

The 1999 is considered among the finest Sauternes of its year, produced in conditions that were ideal for botrytis development. It has decades of life still ahead.

## Viticulture

Same 113-hectare vineyard on the highest hill in Sauternes. Same Ciron-influenced microclimate generating morning mists. Same multiple selective passes harvesting only perfectly botrytized grapes. Same uncompromising standards.

## Winemaking

Fermentation in 100% new French oak barrels. Extended barrel ageing. The 1999 benefited from excellent botrytis conditions that allowed for perfectly concentrated, noble-rot-affected grapes.

## Tasting Notes

At 27 years of age, the 1999 Yquem is a monument of Sauternes. Deep gold to amber colour. The nose unfolds layers of dried tropical fruit, candied orange, toasted almond, caramel, saffron, tobacco and dried flowers. On the palate it is majestic, with an unctuously rich texture perfectly counterbalanced by bright, cleansing acidity. Flavours of marmalade, honeycomb, roasted nuts and spice persist for minutes on the finish.

## Vintage 1999

1999 is considered one of the great Sauternes vintages of the modern era. Ideal botrytis conditions produced wines of extraordinary richness and concentration. The warmth of the year provided generous fruit, while the Ciron's mists ensured noble rot developed evenly and cleanly. At 27 years of age, the 1999 Yquem has undergone a complete transformation: the primary tropical fruit of its youth has evolved into dried fruit, caramel, tobacco and dried flowers. It will continue to evolve for decades. Yquem from the 18th and 19th centuries remains drinkable today, so this bottle has virtually unlimited potential.
