## The Producer

The same estate as above, but the 2003 tells a very different story. The summer of 2003 brought an extreme heatwave across Europe that devastated many vineyards, and Merlot on the Right Bank suffered more than most. Pomerol estates saw widespread problems: overripe fruit, unbalanced wines, heat-stressed vines. Many of the region's finest names produced disappointing wines that year.

Pétrus did not. And the reason is, again, that blue clay. While other Pomerol vineyards on gravel and sand lost moisture rapidly in the scorching heat, Pétrus's deep clay subsoil retained water like a sponge, keeping the vines hydrated and the fruit balanced when everything around it struggled. The 2003 Pétrus emerged as one of the great successes of the vintage, a wine that defies the very circumstances of its creation. It is a testament not just to the winemaking team (Jean-Claude Berrouet and Christian Moueix) but to the terroir itself: a vineyard so perfectly suited to its grape variety that it can produce greatness even in the most extreme conditions.

## Viticulture

Same vineyard, same blue clay, same 100% Merlot. But in 2003 the terroir proved its worth under pressure: the dense clay retained moisture during the record heatwave, keeping vines hydrated when neighbouring vineyards on lighter soils suffered catastrophically.

## Winemaking

Fermentation in concrete vats with careful temperature management. Aged in approximately 50% new French oak for 18 to 20 months. The experience of the Moueix/Berrouet team proved decisive in navigating the extreme conditions of the vintage.

## Tasting Notes

One of the great successes of 2003 in Pomerol. Deep garnet hue. Complex bouquet of truffles, dark chocolate, black cherry, roasted coffee, figs and black cherry jam. Gloriously vivid, with extraordinary freshness that always surprises in this very hot vintage. On the palate it is powerfully huge, but the fruit is so rich and superb that the tannins become part of a complete whole. Dense, minerally, with a fleshy generosity and a long, mouth-filling finish.

## Vintage 2003

The 2003 European heatwave was catastrophic for many Pomerol estates. Merlot, with its thin skins and preference for cooler conditions, suffered terribly on light, sandy and gravelly soils that dried out rapidly. Many Right Bank wines from that year are overripe, unbalanced or simply disappointing. Pétrus was a remarkable exception. Wine Enthusiast called it "one of the great successes of 2003, and a surprise given the problems with Merlot in Pomerol." The explanation lies in the blue clay: while neighbouring vineyards on lighter soils lost moisture catastrophically, Pétrus's dense clay subsoil acted as a reservoir, keeping roots hydrated through the worst of the heat. Sotheby's tasting notes describe "a gloriously vivid, almost rose-hip nose and extraordinary freshness, which is always the surprise in this very hot vintage."
