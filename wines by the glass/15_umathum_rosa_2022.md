## The Producer

Josef "Pepi" Umathum is Austria's great champion of Zweigelt and the dark-skinned indigenous varieties that most of the world overlooks in favour of the country's more famous whites. A former landscape architecture student, Pepi jumped at the chance to take over his parents' winery (founded in 1958 near the vast Lake Neusiedlersee) and has since grown it from 10 to nearly 90 acres.

His farming is deeply principled. He has practiced selection massale (propagating vines from the entire vineyard rather than selected clones) since the late 1980s, converted to biodynamics in 2005, and now keeps a flock of sheep in the vineyards for manure and pest control. The estate is solar-powered.

Rosa is not a typical summer rosé. It is Pepi's idea of what a rosé can be when made from serious, thick-skinned red grapes with real substance: darker, more structured, almost a light red. He calls it the "perfect winter rosé," and it drinks like a basket of forest berries and spiced cherries.

## Viticulture

From vines up to 46 years old near Lake Neusiedlersee in Burgenland. Equal proportions of Zweigelt, Sankt Laurent and Blaufränkisch, all indigenous Austrian varieties grown biodynamically. The Pannonian climate with hot, dry summers produces grapes of excellent concentration and intensity.

## Winemaking

The Rosa is produced using the saignée method: juice is "bled off" from red grapes after skin maceration and before full fermentation. The must is run off the mash with a pink colour and fermented without pressing in stainless steel tanks to preserve freshness. The wine is aged in stainless steel, maintaining purity and vibrancy. Minimal intervention throughout.

## Tasting Notes

Darker and more substantial than typical rosés, with a deep pink colour from the thick-skinned grapes. On the nose and palate it offers lively acidity that marks cherry, strawberry and stony mineral notes. Details of heather and spice elongate the finish. It drinks almost like a light, chillable red, tasting like a basket of forest berries and spiced cherries.

## Vintage 2022

Burgenland's 2022 was warm and dry, consistent with the broader European pattern that year. For Umathum's biodynamically farmed old vines (up to 46 years old), the warmth concentrated the fruit beautifully. The saignée method draws juice off before full fermentation begins, meaning the growing season's concentration translates into more intense colour and fruit character in the rosé. The stainless steel ageing then preserves the lively acidity that prevents the wine from becoming heavy despite its deeper, more substantial character.
