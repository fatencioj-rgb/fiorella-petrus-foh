## The Producer

Tokaji Aszú is the world's oldest documented sweet wine, with records dating back to the 17th century. Louis XIV called it "the wine of kings and the king of wines." Royal Tokaji was founded in 1990 to restore this legacy after decades of communist-era neglect, when Hungary's greatest wines were reduced to industrial production for Soviet bloc markets.

The company holds parcels in several of Tokaj's classed growth vineyards (classified in 1700, over a century before Bordeaux's 1855 classification). The region's volcanic rhyolite soils, unique microclimate and indigenous grape varieties combine with subterranean cellars covered in a thick layer of beneficial mould (cladosporium cellare) to create wines that exist nowhere else on earth.

The 2018 was exceptional: the quality and size of the Aszú harvest allowed Royal Tokaji to make 6 Puttonyos wines from all five of their classed growth vineyards for the first time in a decade. The 5 Puttonyos designation indicates at least 120 g/l of residual sugar, achieved entirely through noble rot.

## Viticulture

Volcanic rhyolite soils in the Tokaj region. Rich volcanic clay with a unique microclimate ideal for botrytis cinerea (noble rot). Indigenous grape varieties Furmint and Hárslevelű, selected as Aszú berries (individually picked, botrytis-affected grapes).

## Winemaking

Aszú berries are macerated into a concentrated base wine. Aged in Hungarian oak barrels (approximately 40% new for this vintage) for two years before bottling. The solera-like cellar environment, with its thick coating of beneficial mould, contributes to the unique character of these wines.

## Tasting Notes

Deep gold and vibrant. Intensely aromatic with outstanding purity of papaya and stone fruit, lifted orange zest, honey and sweet blossom. Medium- to full-bodied with vivid, mouthwatering acidity and a dense, creamy texture. Bright and harmonious, integrating pureed apricot, dried raspberry, orange marmalade, praline and crystallized honey. The finish is long, elegant and expressive.

## Vintage 2018

The 2018 harvest in Tokaj was exceptional in both quality and size. Royal Tokaji was able to make 6 Puttonyos wines from all five of their classed growth vineyards for the first time in a decade, a testament to how uniformly excellent the botrytis development was across the region. The volcanic rhyolite soils and the unique microclimate of the Tokaj hills (warm days, cool nights from the Carpathian foothills, and morning mists from the Bodrog river) combined perfectly in 2018. The wines were aged in Hungarian oak (40% new) for two years before bottling in June 2021.
