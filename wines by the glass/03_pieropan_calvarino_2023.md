## The Producer

Pieropan is the family that single-handedly rescued Soave's reputation. In a world where Soave had become synonymous with cheap, industrial white wine, Leonildo Pieropan was the first to bottle a single-vineyard Soave, releasing the inaugural Calvarino in 1971. It was a radical act: proving that this appellation, on volcanic soils with ancient vines, could produce wines of genuine complexity and longevity.

Now in its fourth generation, the estate remains a reference point for what Italian white wine can be. The Calvarino vineyard (whose name means "little Calvary," a reference to the steep, punishing terrain) is the spiritual heart of the domaine. It sits alongside La Rocca as one of two single-vineyard wines that have earned Pieropan distinction far beyond the peninsula. All farming is now certified organic.

## Viticulture

The Calvarino vineyard sits at approximately 250 metres above sea level within the Colombara UGA of the Soave Classico zone. The soils are volcanic basalt with limestone components, a geological profile that imparts distinctive mineral tension and depth. The vineyard is south-facing with steep slopes that Leonildo Pieropan first planted over 60 years ago. The combination of altitude, volcanic soils and old vines creates a wine of a completely different register from generic Soave.

## Winemaking

Grapes are hand-harvested and undergo gentle whole-cluster pressing. The must is fermented and aged in tulip-shaped, glass-lined concrete tanks, which provide a neutral environment that preserves varietal character while allowing slow, gentle micro-oxygenation. The wine rests on its fine lees for approximately 12 months, building complexity and textural depth before bottling. No oak is used.

## Tasting Notes

Pale straw yellow with greenish reflections when young. The nose is beautifully complex, with aromas of white spring wildflower, honey, citrus (lemon zest), wet flint, Alpine herbs and stone fruit (apricot, poached apple). On the palate the wine shows textural heft and a silky approach, wrapping smoothly across the palate with flavours of green apple, almond, blossom and oyster shell. The finish is chalky and persistent, driven by a crystalline mineral freshness.

## Vintage 2023

2023 in the Veneto was erratic and atypical. Cooler temperatures in April and May eventually gave way to heat, but rain persisted throughout much of the growing season, reaching an unparalleled peak in July, particularly in the Classico zone. The month also brought localised hailstorms of varying intensity. However, the cooler temperatures ultimately benefited Garganega and Trebbiano, which need time to develop their complex aromatics. The slightly late harvest, combined with the volcanic soils' excellent drainage, meant that producers on the best sites (like Pieropan's elevated Calvarino) came through the difficult year with concentrated, focused wines while lower-lying vineyards struggled.
