## The Producer

The 40 Year Old is the pinnacle of Graham's aged Tawny range, and it represents something close to the limit of what oxidative ageing can achieve. After four decades in small oak casks, the wine has been transformed so completely from its original state that it bears almost no resemblance to the young, fiery ruby Port it once was. What remains is pure essence: concentrated, ethereal, almost timeless.

To create a 40 Year Old Tawny, the blender selects from the oldest reserves in the lodge, wines that have been slowly evaporating and concentrating for decades. The loss to evaporation (the "angel's share") means these old casks contain very little liquid, but what remains is of extraordinary intensity. The art is in the blending: assembling wines from different decades to create something that tastes not of a specific year but of accumulated time itself.

## Winemaking & Ageing

After fortification, wines are aged in small oak pipes for an average of 40 years in the lodges of Vila Nova de Gaia. This exceptional length of ageing concentrates flavours to an extraordinary degree through slow evaporation and oxidation. The blend is carefully assembled from the oldest reserves to represent the ultimate expression of Graham's Tawny style.

## Tasting Notes

Deep golden amber with olive-green rim. Extraordinary aromatic complexity: honeyed nuts, dried exotic fruits, candied orange, dark toffee, sandalwood, cinnamon and aged balsamic. On the palate it is ethereally smooth and concentrated, with layers upon layers of flavour unfolding. The finish lingers for minutes.
