## The Producer

This is the same Pieropan estate that produces the Calvarino, but Le Colombare represents something entirely different: an ancient winemaking tradition that predates Soave as we know it. Recioto was actually the original wine of this area, made for centuries before anyone thought to produce dry white Soave. The name comes from "recie" (ears), referring to the upper wings of the grape cluster that receive the most sun and are selected for drying.

Le Colombare is only produced in the best years, making it genuinely rare. From old-vine Garganega at 300 metres on volcanic soils, organically farmed, hand-picked and dried on bamboo mats. This is a wine that connects you to a tradition stretching back centuries, when sweet wine was not a niche product but the very definition of quality winemaking.

## Viticulture

Old-vine Garganega grown at 300 metres above sea level on volcanic soils within the Soave Classico zone. Organic farming. Only made in exceptional years, when the fruit achieves the concentration and health needed for the appassimento process.

## Winemaking

Grapes are hand-picked and then dried on bamboo mats (appassimento method), slowly dehydrating to intensify sugars, acids and flavours. After several months of drying, a slow fermentation follows. The wine then undergoes extended ageing, building the complexity, spiced character and tropical-fruit depth that define great Recioto.

## Tasting Notes

A rare sweet treat. Pineapple, tinned peaches, orange peel and marzipan dance out of the glass. The richness and sweetness on the palate are counteracted by vibrant acidity that prevents the wine from being cloying. Dried apricot, baked apple, citrus zest, roasted hazelnut and honey emerge on the long, complex finish.

## Vintage 2021

A favourable year for passito production. Cool conditions preserved excellent acidity in the fruit prior to the drying process, providing the essential backbone that balances the concentrated sweetness of the finished wine.
