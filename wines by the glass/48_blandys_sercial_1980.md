## The Producer

Blandy's is the only family of all the original founders of the Madeira wine trade to still be involved in production and management. They have been making wine on this volcanic Atlantic island since 1811, and the family name has become virtually synonymous with Madeira itself.

Sercial is the driest of Madeira's four noble grape varieties, and arguably the most thrilling. Grown at the island's highest, coolest vineyards (up to 800 metres), it produces grapes of screamingly high natural acidity that, when fortified and aged for decades, creates wines of breathtaking vitality and seemingly immortal freshness. A 1980 Sercial has spent over 40 years in cask, yet tastes as alive and energetic as a wine a fraction of its age.

This is what makes Madeira unique among all the world's wines: the combination of heat (the canteiro ageing method uses natural warmth) and acidity means these wines are essentially indestructible. Bottles from the 18th and 19th centuries remain not just drinkable but extraordinary today.

## Viticulture

Sercial grown at the island's highest, coolest vineyards on volcanic soils. The extreme altitude produces grapes of very high natural acidity, the defining characteristic that gives old Madeira its seemingly eternal freshness.

## Winemaking & Ageing

Fortified after fermentation (allowing nearly all sugar to be consumed, hence the dry style). Aged for over 40 years in seasoned oak casks using the canteiro method (natural warming in heated lofts). The Frasqueira (vintage) designation requires a minimum of 20 years in cask; this wine far exceeds that threshold.

## Tasting Notes

Amber-golden colour with olive rim. Extraordinarily complex nose of toasted almonds, citrus peel, dried herbs, caramel, smoke, sea salt and iodine. On the palate bone-dry, with searing acidity and incredibly concentrated flavours of roasted nuts, orange zest, coffee and spice. The finish is endless. A wine of breathtaking vitality for its age.

## Vintage 1980

Over 40 years of ageing in cask. This Frasqueira Madeira represents the pinnacle of the Sercial style: virtually indestructible, and will continue to evolve for decades to come.
