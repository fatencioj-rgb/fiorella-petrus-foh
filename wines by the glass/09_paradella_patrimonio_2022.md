## The Producer

Patrimonio is Corsica's oldest and most prestigious wine appellation, established in 1968 as the island's first AOC. It is a place where French and Italian winemaking traditions collide on a Mediterranean island with its own fierce identity. Here, Vermentino (called Vermentinu in Corsican) is the only permitted white grape, and it reaches an expression found nowhere else: fuller, more mineral and more structured than its counterparts on the Italian mainland or Sardinia.

Domaine Paradella works within this singular terroir. The appellation's clay-limestone soils give the wines their distinctive structure and mineral complexity, while the Mediterranean climate tempered by coastal breezes provides the warmth for generous fruit alongside the cool evenings needed for balanced acidity. It is island winemaking in the truest sense: defined by place, shaped by the sea.

## Viticulture

Clay-limestone soils characterise the Patrimonio appellation, providing the mineral backbone that distinguishes these wines. The Mediterranean climate benefits from maritime influence and proximity to coastal lakes that create a microclimate preserving natural acidity. Warm days ripen the fruit generously while cool nights maintain freshness.

## Winemaking

The grapes are carefully harvested and gently pressed to extract clean, aromatic juice. Fermentation is temperature-controlled in stainless steel tanks to preserve the fresh, aromatic character of Vermentino. The wine is kept on fine lees for several months to build texture and complexity. The winemaking philosophy prioritises terroir expression and the grape's natural aromatic profile, with minimal intervention.

## Tasting Notes

The wine displays a bright, pale gold colour. The nose is pronounced, with characteristic Vermentino aromas of rose petals, citrus fruit (lemon, grapefruit), exotic fruit and fresh herbs. On the palate it is full and generous, with a rounded texture, good concentration and a saline mineral backbone that reflects the limestone terroir. The finish is long and refreshing, with a subtle bitter almond note typical of the grape variety.

## Vintage 2022

The 2022 vintage in Corsica was exceptionally dry, with harvests in Haute-Corse (northern Corsica, where Patrimonio is located) beginning at the start of August, one to three weeks earlier than usual. France 24 reported this as a direct consequence of climate adaptation across French wine regions. The dryness brought concentration to the wines, and the clay-limestone soils of Patrimonio, which retain moisture better than the granite of other Corsican appellations, helped buffer the vines against the worst effects of drought. The resulting whites show good concentration and structure from the small, thick-skinned berries.
