## The Producer

Pétrus is, simply, the most famous Merlot in the world. It carries no classification (Pomerol has none) yet outprices every First Growth in the Médoc. There is no château building, no grand entrance, no corporate ownership. Just 11.4 hectares of vineyard, a modest winery, and one wine.

What makes Pétrus singular is beneath the surface. The vineyard sits at the highest point of the Pomerol plateau on a seam of blue clay that is approximately 40 million years old, laced with iron-rich deposits known locally as crasse de fer. This clay exists nowhere else in the wine world in such concentration. The entire Pétrus vineyard sits directly on top of it, while neighbouring properties may have only a portion. The clay is so dense and hard that roots cannot penetrate it, instead spreading laterally and drawing mineral complexity from its surface.

The Moueix family has owned and managed the property since 1970. Jean-Claude Berrouet was cellar master for over 40 years (1964-2007), succeeded by his son Olivier. Only Merlot is grown. Only one wine is made. There is no second label. Everything that is not good enough for Pétrus simply does not become Pétrus.

## Viticulture

The vineyard sits at the highest point of the Pomerol plateau, approximately 40 metres above sea level. Two layers of clay: the topsoil of dark clay is 60 to 80 centimetres thick, and below it lies the unique subsoil of very dense, deep, dark blue clay not found in any other vineyard. 100% Merlot. Hand-harvested with meticulous sorting.

## Winemaking

Fermentation in concrete vats. Ageing in 50% new French oak barrels for approximately 18 to 20 months. The winemaking is one of precision and restraint, allowing the extraordinary terroir to express itself fully.

## Tasting Notes

The wine opens with an extremely precise, controlled bouquet, layering ripe black cherries, truffle and crystalline red and black fruit. More mineral character emerges than in some recent vintages. On the palate it reveals beautiful mid-palate depth, sweet tannins, and a voluptuous yet weightless texture that needs to be tasted to be believed. A masterclass in balance, reconciling strength with finesse.

## Vintage 2015

Pomerol's 2015 was exceptional for Merlot. Moderated heat (not the extremes of 2003 or 2022) coupled with timely precipitation produced conditions many growers described as ideal. The Right Bank excelled particularly that year, with Pomerol widely considered its strongest appellation. The Wine Cellar Insider described the best 2015 Pomerols as "bottled decadence, hedonistic thrill rides of almost unequalled sensual pleasure," comparing them to the best 2009s. For Pétrus specifically, the 2015 was aged in 50% new oak, and tasters noted more mineral character than in some recent vintages, perhaps because there was less "puppy fat" obscuring the terroir expression.
