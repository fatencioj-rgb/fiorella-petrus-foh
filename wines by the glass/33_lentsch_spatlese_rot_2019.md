## The Producer

Sweet red wine is one of Austria's least-known specialties, and Weingut Lentsch is among the producers keeping this tradition alive. Located in the Neusiedlersee region, where the misty autumn influence of Lake Neusiedl creates ideal conditions for late-harvest wines of all colours, Lentsch produces a Spätlese Rot that is unlike anything most people have encountered.

The concept is simple but unusual: take thick-skinned red grapes, allow them to hang late on the vine until sugars concentrate naturally, then vinify gently to produce a sweet wine from red varieties. The result sits somewhere between a dessert wine and a light red, with the berry fruit character of one and the sweetness of the other. It is a style that defies easy categorisation, which is precisely what makes it interesting.

## Viticulture

Neusiedlersee region on the eastern shore of Lake Neusiedl. The Pannonian climate with hot, dry summers allows the red grapes to reach high sugar levels for late-harvest style. The lake's influence on microclimate extends the ripening season.

## Winemaking

Late-harvested red grapes with elevated natural sugar levels. Gentle extraction preserving fruit character and natural sweetness. Limited skin contact maintains elegance. The wine balances residual sugar with the natural acidity of the fruit.

## Tasting Notes

Sweet red with aromas of raisin, fig, dark berry fruit, vanilla and spice. Juicy and rich on the palate, with concentrated dark fruit sweetness balanced by a good level of acidity that provides freshness and prevents heaviness. Warm, lingering finish.
