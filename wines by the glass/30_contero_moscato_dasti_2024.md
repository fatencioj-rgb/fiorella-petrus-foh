## The Producer

Contero is based in Strevi, in the province of Alessandria, a village historically considered one of the finest areas for Moscato cultivation in all of Piedmont. The vines here are superbly tended and produce fruit of stunning quality. While Moscato d'Asti is sometimes dismissed as simple party wine, producers like Contero remind you what this style can be when taken seriously: the Moscato is lifted, fresh and frothing, with a lovely purity that only comes from excellent fruit.

At just 5.5% alcohol, Moscato d'Asti occupies a unique position in the wine world. It is not a wine for contemplation; it is a wine for joy. The low alcohol, the gentle sweetness, the delicate sparkle, all of it designed to be immediate, effortless pleasure. But behind that simplicity is a winemaking process that requires real precision to achieve.

## Viticulture

Moscato Bianco (Muscat Blanc à Petits Grains), one of the world's most aromatic grape varieties, grown on the hillsides of Strevi. The terroir benefits from the continental climate of Piedmont moderated by the Langhe hills.

## Winemaking

Grapes are gently pressed and fermented at low temperatures to maximise fragrance. During fermentation the must is filtered several times. The wine is drawn from the vat at just 5.5% alcohol, then stabilised and bottled under pressure to maintain the slight sparkle. This single cool tank fermentation locks in natural CO2 and residual sweetness while preserving the grape's head-turning aromatics.

## Tasting Notes

Lifted, fresh and frothing. Delicate notes of peach, sage, lime blossom, white flowers and fresh grape. Light, sweet and effervescent, with bright acidity providing a refreshing counterpoint to the residual sugar. The low alcohol makes it an easy, joyful wine.

## Vintage 2024

After two particularly poor vintages of rainfall, 2024 brought welcome rains from late February onward with above-average temperatures, favouring healthy vine development and excellent aromatic expression in the Moscato grapes.
