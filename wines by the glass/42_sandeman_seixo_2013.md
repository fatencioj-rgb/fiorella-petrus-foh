## The Producer

Sandeman is one of Port's most iconic brands, recognisable by its Don (the mysterious cloaked figure in a Portuguese student's cape and a Spanish hat) created in 1928. The house was founded in 1790 by George Sandeman, a Scotsman who came to London with £300 from his father and a determination to build a wine business.

Quinta do Seixo is Sandeman's flagship estate, located in the prestigious Cima Corgo sub-region of the Douro. The name references the stones (seixos) found throughout the vineyard, a detail that says everything about the terroir: schist and stone, heat and slope, the raw materials from which Port is built. A Vintage Port from a single year offers the complexity and concentration of a declared vintage in a more accessible format.

## Viticulture

Terraced vineyards on schist soils in the Cima Corgo, overlooking the Douro river. The estate's stony character contributes to the concentration and mineral backbone of the wines.

## Winemaking

Traditional Port vinification: grapes are trodden, fermentation halted by fortification with grape spirit. Aged in oak casks before bottling as a single-vintage wine.

## Tasting Notes

Deep colour with youthful purple hues. Intense aromas of black fruit, violets and spice. Full and rich on the palate with concentrated fruit, firm structure and a long, balanced finish.
